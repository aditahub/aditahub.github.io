About Me

I’m passionate about exploring the intersection of artificial intelligence and human health. With a background in clinical psychology and hands-on experience in AI modeling and medical image analysis, I bring a unique, multidisciplinary perspective to research and innovation.

AI & Research

Currently working at the Fetal-Neonatal Neuroimaging and Developmental Science Center (FNNDSC) at Boston Children's Hospital, I train deep learning models to segment fetal MRI images and evaluate their performance. My recent projects include testing AI pipelines for fetal and pediatric eye segmentation and exploring cross-modality generalization challenges in medical imaging.

Clinical Psychology Background

My clinical training includes a Master’s in Clinical Psychology from the Hebrew University and a Bachelor’s in Psychology and Philosophy from Tel Aviv University. I’ve worked in hospital and rehabilitation as well as research settings, where understanding human behavior, cognition, and emotion has deeply influenced how I think about AI, ethics, and user-centered design.

Learning Philosophy

I’m always eager to expand into new domains and learn more. I believe that blending diverse perspectives is essential to solving real-world problems.
